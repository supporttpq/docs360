# Overview

The Integrations framework in Tourpaq connects the platform with external systems, suppliers, financial services, communication providers, and travel industry partners. These integrations allow information to flow automatically between Tourpaq and third-party systems, reducing manual work and ensuring that operational data remains synchronized.

Integrations are used throughout the Tourpaq ecosystem, supporting hotel providers, transport providers, GDS systems, payment gateways, accounting systems, CRM platforms, marketing tools, insurance providers, and supplier communication services. They enable Tourpaq to function as a central operational platform while maintaining connectivity with external partners and services. ### Purpose

The purpose of integrations is to:

* Automate the exchange of booking, pricing, customer, and operational data.
* Reduce manual administration and duplicate data entry.
* Synchronize information between Tourpaq and external providers.
* Improve booking accuracy and operational efficiency.
* Support dynamic packaging and real-time availability.
* Streamline financial and communication workflows.
* Ensure suppliers and partners receive timely information required to deliver services. ([tourpaq.com](https://www.tourpaq.com/features-category/integrations?utm_source=chatgpt.com))

### Integration Categories

Tourpaq supports several categories of integrations.

#### Hotel Provider Integrations

Hotel provider integrations allow Tourpaq to retrieve and synchronize hotel data from external bed banks and accommodation suppliers.

Typical functionality includes:

* Hotel availability
* Hotel pricing
* Hotel images
* Hotel content synchronization
* Reservation management

Examples include:

* HotelBeds
* D-EDGE (Availpro)
* SkiStar Resort integrations

These integrations help ensure that accommodation data remains updated and available for booking flows.&#x20;

#### Transport and GDS Integrations

Transport integrations connect Tourpaq with external transport providers and Global Distribution Systems (GDS).

Typical functionality includes:

* Flight search
* Availability retrieval
* Reservation creation
* Passenger reporting
* Schedule synchronization

Examples include:

* Galileo
* Travelport
* Amadeus
* Paxport
* RailHub

These integrations support both traditional package travel and dynamic packaging workflows.

### How Integrations Work

Most integrations follow a common process:

1. An external provider is configured in System Setup.
2. Authentication credentials are entered.
3. Tourpaq establishes communication with the provider.
4. Data is exchanged through APIs, files, scheduled services, or automated synchronization jobs.
5. Information becomes available throughout the booking, reporting, and operational workflows.&#x20;

Depending on the provider, integrations may operate in:

* Real-time
* Scheduled synchronization intervals
* Manual import/export workflows
* Automated communication services

***

### Example

#### HotelBeds Availability Synchronization

1. HotelBeds credentials are configured in System Setup.
2. Tourpaq connects to the HotelBeds API.
3. Hotel availability and pricing are retrieved.
4. Data becomes available in booking searches.
5. Updates continue to synchronize automatically according to the integration configuration.&#x20;

#### Airline Passenger Reporting

1. A transport reporting method is configured.
2. A communication scheduler is defined.
3. The Communication Service generates passenger information.
4. Passenger lists are automatically sent to the airline according to the configured schedule.&#x20;
